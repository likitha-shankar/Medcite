--- DAY 1-2: STABILISATION PHASE ---

Mr. Wilson was admitted directly to the Coronary Care Unit (CCU) given haemodynamic
instability, oxygen desaturation, and hyperkalemia.

Immediate management:

Hyperkalemia (K+ 6.1):
- Calcium gluconate 10 mL of 10% solution IV administered for cardiac membrane
  stabilisation — repeat ECG post-administration showed no peaked T-waves, confirming
  no immediate lethal arrhythmia risk
- Insulin-dextrose infusion (10 units actrapid in 50 mL 50% dextrose over 30 minutes)
- Sodium zirconium cyclosilicate (Lokelma) 10g orally three times daily commenced for
  ongoing potassium removal
- Ramipril held given AKI and hyperkalemia
- Eplerenone held given hyperkalemia

Acute Decompensated Heart Failure:
- Furosemide 80mg IV bolus administered on arrival, then 40mg IV twice daily
- Fluid restriction: 1.5L per 24 hours
- Sodium restriction: <2g per day — dietary consult ordered
- Daily weights commenced (target: negative fluid balance of 1-1.5L per day)
- Strict fluid balance charting
- Supplemental oxygen: initially 4L/min nasal cannula, weaned to room air by Day 3

Atrial Fibrillation with Rapid Ventricular Response:
- Rate control strategy adopted given chronic AF (patient has known paroxysmal AF,
  now appears to be persistent)
- Carvedilol dose uptitrated cautiously — held initially given haemodynamic instability,
  reintroduced Day 2 at 6.25mg BD
- Digoxin 0.25mg IV loading dose given on Day 1, followed by 0.0625mg oral daily
  (renally adjusted given AKI) — achieved rate control with HR 68-78 bpm by Day 3
- Anticoagulation: LMWH (enoxaparin 40mg SC daily, renally adjusted) commenced
  for AF; transitioned to apixaban 2.5mg BD at discharge (dose-reduced for age,
  weight, and renal function — meets 2-of-3 criteria for dose reduction)

Hypertension:
- IV labetalol avoided given decompensated HF
- Hypertension managed conservatively with diuresis; BP responded to decongestion
- Amlodipine 5mg orally once daily added Day 3 for residual hypertension
  (cautious use given oedema; justified by BP 168/96 despite diuresis)

Blood Glucose Management:
- Metformin HELD on admission (AKI + contrast study planned)
- Insulin glargine continued at 28 units; correction doses of insulin aspart adjusted
- Endocrinology contacted — recommended increasing basal insulin to 32 units and
  optimising meal-time insulin with carbohydrate counting education
- Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM — FreeStyle Libre) reviewed by diabetes nurse
